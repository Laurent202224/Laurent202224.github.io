## Full-Time Robotics Engineer, Avientus
**Sep 2025 - Mar 2026**

During my time at Avientus, I worked on the development of a fully autonomous hexacopter designed for last-mile delivery and search-and-rescue missions in GNSS-denied environments.

I was responsible for the full planning and decision-making pipeline, leveraging SLAM-based navigation to enable autonomous operation in unknown environments. In addition, I developed the control architecture based on the Pixhawk flight controller.

As the platform was built from scratch for the [SPRIND "FUNKE Fully Autonomous Flight 2.0"](https://www.sprind.org/taten/challenges/funke-fully-autonomous-flight-2.0) challenge, I was also heavily involved in hardware integration. This included working with a Jetson Orin AGX (64GB) and integrating multiple sensors such as radars, cameras, optical flow sensors, and IMUs.

Our team successfully advanced to the next stage of the competition and was awarded €325k in funding.

Following this, I focused on simulation-based development of a VTOL drone in Gazebo. I implemented autonomous takeoff starting in fixed-wing configuration and transitioning to vertical flight using only two tilt rotors. This required developing a custom flight mode for the Pixhawk flight controller.

## Electronics Engineer, ARIS (NAUTILUS Project)
**Oct 2024 - Mar 2025**

As part of the ARIS student project team, I worked on the NAUTILUS unmanned underwater vehicle (UUV).

I was responsible for designing and implementing the system regulating the flow and reflow between the tank and the bladder. The solution was successfully tested and validated on real hardware.
