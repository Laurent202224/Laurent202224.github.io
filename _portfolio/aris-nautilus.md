In this project, I worked as an electrical engineer on an autonomous underwater vehicle (UUV) designed to collect environmental samples in the Antarctic, contributing to research on the impact of climate change.

My primary responsibility was the development and control of the vehicle’s buoyancy regulation system. Specifically, I designed and implemented the control of electro-mechanical valves managing the flow and reflow of oil between a tank and a bladder. By precisely regulating this fluid transfer, we were able to adjust the vehicle’s center of mass and buoyancy, enabling controlled vertical motion (ascent and descent) in the water column.

This work was critical for ensuring stable and reliable depth control, a key requirement for accurate sampling and mission success in extreme environments.
