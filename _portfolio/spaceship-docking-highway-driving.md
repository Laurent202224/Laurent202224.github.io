These projects focused on the implementation of advanced optimization-based control algorithms in dynamic and constrained environments.

In the Spaceship Docking project, we applied the Successive Convexification (SCvx) for Non-convex Optimal Control algorithm to compute time-optimal trajectories for a spacecraft to dock with a space station. The solution accounted for both static and dynamic obstacles, such as planets and satellites, while satisfying system dynamics and safety constraints.

In the Highway Driving project, we designed a similar control framework for an autonomous vehicle operating in dense traffic conditions. The controller enabled safe lane changes, collision avoidance, and efficient navigation toward a target destination, balancing safety and performance in a highly dynamic environment.

Together, these projects emphasized the practical application of state-of-the-art control and trajectory optimization techniques to real-world-inspired scenarios.
